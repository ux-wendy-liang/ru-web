The Context

A Dashboard Everyone Visits, But Nobody Can Explore

LinkedIn's internal data tool (Retina) helps PMs, engineers, and leadership track product metrics. The dashboard was one of the most visited pages.

The problem: Users couldn't filter data on the dashboard itself. To explore data, they had to click into each chart individually — then mentally piece the story together.

The result? PMs couldn't self-serve. They constantly asked data scientists to pull numbers — turning data creators into "human filters."

The Turning Point

Engineering Had a Solution. I Thought We Were Solving the Wrong Problem.

I made the case to both the PM and engineering manager: "We have 8 engineers ready to build. Give me 2 weeks to make sure we build the right thing." I framed it as risk reduction — 2 weeks of research costs far less than shipping a wrong solution. They agreed.

Research

Uncovering What Users Actually Needed

I designed a research study targeting two distinct user groups — because I hypothesized that creators and consumers had fundamentally different pain points.

I conducted 6 one-on-one interviews and identified a core dynamic:

"The tool is too technical for consumer self-discovery, which often leads to the producer being a human filter."

— From user research interviews

This reframed the entire project. The problem wasn't missing filters — the tool was designed for data scientists, not for the PMs who needed it most.

From Insight to Strategy

One Insight That Changed the Project Direction

"How might we enable creators to build intuitive dashboards while empowering consumers to self-serve?"

The Solution
